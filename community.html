Community Characteristics

Diversity at MCHD is not a number but a dynamic experience of rich interactions among our students, faculty, staff, and families in the classroom, on the playground, and at organized school events. This page offers a current snapshot of the unique atmosphere within which our students learn about each other, their community, and the world.

Community Building

The MCHD classroom is an intimate setting where diversity is not only encountered but explored through:

  • group lessons,
  • individual work plans,
  • classrom events,
  • and parent sharings.

Because our teachers have a longer tenure at MCHD than is typical for schools, our community is able to benefit from

  • the diverse backgrounds of our teachers
  • the maturation of keen observance skills inherent to the Montessori Philosophy
  • a continuity in school priorities year to year such as our Diversity recruitment and retention efforts.

Currently, in an effort to foster cultural competency, our faculty and staff are participating in the Teaching Diverse Students Initiative, a project of the Southern Poverty Law Center.

MCHD enrichment extends beyond the classroom into organized school events which seek to foster a sincere bond between families.

We devote the same unparalleled intensity in cultivating an atmosphere of inclusion, care and respect for our families as we do for our students.

Some refer to this experience as school spirit; others, a welcoming culture. The best explanation, however, is that we have true community at MCHD that thrives on the diversity within.
